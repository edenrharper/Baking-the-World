Chocolate Cake

There's nothing better then taking a bit of a nice, rich, and fudgy chocolate cake. Fun fact, did you know that the secret ingredient to creating tons of richness in chocolate cake is mayonnaise.

Ingredients

Chocolate cake

  • 1 3/4 cups (230g) all purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup (30g) cornstarch
  • 1 cup (100g) unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 2 tsp baking soda
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp kosher salt
  • 1 cup (200g) granulated sugar
  • 1 cup (205g) dark brown sugar, packed
  • 1/2 cup (108g) vegetable oil
  • 1 cup (230g) mayonnaise
  • 3 large eggs + 1 egg yolk
  • 1/2 cup (128g) milk
  • 1 tbsp vinegar
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 cup (240g) hot coffee (or 1 cup hot water + 2 tsp instant espresso powder)

Chocolate Fudge Frosting

  • 2 cups (450g) unsalted butter, room temp
  • 1 1/2 cups (165g) powdered sugar
  • 3 tbsp corn syrup
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • Pinch of kosher salt
  • 3/4 cup (177g) warm water
  • 3/4 cup (75g) dutch process cocoa powder (or unsweetened cocoa powder)
  • 10 oz dark chocolate chips (one bag), melted and cooled slightly

Instructions

Chocolate Cake

  1. Preheat the oven to 350F and prep 3 8″ cake pans. Grease the inside and line the bottom with parchment paper. If you have them, wrap the pans in damp cake strips and set aside.
  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, cornstarch, cocoa powder, baking soda, baking powder, and salt. Set aside.
  3. In a measuring glass, add the milk and stir in the vinegar and vanilla. Set aside.
  4. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the sugars, oil, and mayo, followed by the eggs and egg yolk.
  5. Mix in the milk mixture.
  6. Pour in half of the dry ingredients and whisk to combine.
  7. Pour in the hot coffee and whisk to combine, followed by the remaining dry ingredients. The batter should be very thin and liquid.
  8. Evenly distribute the batter between the three pans.
  9. Bake for 30-35 minutes or until a toothpick in the center comes out clean with a few moist crumbs.
  10. Allow the cakes to cool in the pan for about 10 minutes, then run a knife around the edges and turn out onto a cooling rack. Cool the cakes completely before frosting, either at room temperature or in the refrigerator.

Chocolate Fudge Frosting

  1. Right when the cakes are done baking, make the frosting.
  2. Using a hand or stand mixer with the paddle attachment, beat the butter until smooth.
  3. Then mix in the powdered sugar, corn syrup, vanilla, and salt.
  4. In a measuring glass, whisk together the warm water and cocoa powder.
  5. Pour into the frosting and mix to combine, scraping down the sides and bottom of the bowl as needed.
  6. Then pour in the melted chocolate and mix to fully combine.
  7. The frosting should now be smooth and glossy but somewhat thin. To thicken, place the bowl in the refrigerator for about 45 minutes.
  8. Once chilled, give the frosting a good mix by hand using a rubber spatula. If the frosting holds its shape inside the bowl, it’s ready to go.
  9. Before assembling, make sure the cake layers are completely cooled.
  10. Level off the top of each cake layer if needed.
  11. Lay one layer down on a cake turntable and spread a layer of frosting on top. Lay the next cake layer on top, repeating the process.
  12. Once all the layers are stacked, place the cake in the refrigerator for about 20 minutes to allow those layers to set. Once set, cover the entire outside of the cake with frosting.
  13. Now slice and enjoy!
